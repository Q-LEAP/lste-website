Call for Speakers

Share your expertise at LSTE 2026 — November 26, Esch-Belval

Submission deadline: September 30, 2026 — Submit early for best consideration

Join the LSTE 2026 Lineup

Join the lineup of LSTE 2026 and share your testing expertise with Luxembourg's QA community. Whether you have a war story from the trenches, a new methodology to showcase, or a tool demo to present — we want to hear from you. The Call for Speakers is open until September 30, 2026 and we invite software testing and quality assurance professionals to share their knowledge with 300+ colleagues.

Talk Formats

Keynote

45 minutes

For thought-provoking big-picture topics. Strategic view, broad appeal, strong storytelling. Keynotes open or close the main programme and set the tone for the day.

Conference Talk

30 minutes

Technical or methodological deep-dives. Case studies, lessons learned, practical frameworks. The backbone of the LSTE programme — show us what you've built or discovered.

Tutorial / Workshop

3 hours

Hands-on learning experiences held in the morning before the conference. Practical skills, interactive exercises. Limited to 20 participants for an intimate learning environment.

Topics We Love

We welcome proposals across the full spectrum of software quality and testing. Here are some areas we're especially excited about this year:

  • Software Testing Strategy
  • Test Automation
  • AI & ML in Testing
  • Quality Engineering
  • DevOps & Continuous Testing
  • Exploratory Testing
  • Performance Testing
  • Security Testing
  • API Testing
  • Mobile Testing
  • Agile Testing
  • Test Management
  • Career Development in QA
  • Testing Tools & Frameworks
  • Quality Metrics

What We Offer Speakers

Free Attendance

All accepted speakers receive complimentary access to the full LSTE 2026 event.

Speaker Networking Dinner

Join fellow speakers and organisers at our exclusive evening dinner the night before the event.

Website & Social Media Feature

Your bio, photo, and talk details will be prominently featured on our website and across our social channels.

Travel Support

We evaluate travel and accommodation support on a case-by-case basis. Please mention your location in your submission.

Submission Guidelines

Please note the following requirements when preparing your proposal:

  • Abstract: 200–400 words describing your talk — what attendees will learn and why the topic matters now.
  • Bio: A 100–150 word speaker biography in the third person.
  • Photo: A professional headshot at high resolution (minimum 800 × 800 px).
  • Vendor-neutral content is strongly preferred. Talks must not be sales pitches or product promotions.
  • First-time speakers are welcome and encouraged. Our programme committee provides feedback and support to help you prepare.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to pay to speak?

No. All speakers receive free admission to the full LSTE 2026 event and are featured on our website and social media channels.

Can I submit more than one proposal?

Yes! We encourage submitting multiple topics. Please send each proposal in a separate email so our programme committee can evaluate them individually.

When will I be notified?

We aim to notify all applicants of the outcome by October 15, 2026. We review proposals on a rolling basis, so submitting early is to your advantage.

Is travel reimbursement available?

We evaluate travel and accommodation support on a case-by-case basis, depending on availability and the distance travelled. Please mention your location clearly in your submission email so we can assess your situation.
