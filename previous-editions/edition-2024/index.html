6th Edition, 14 November 2024

Continuous Quality.

280+ testing and quality professionals gathered at the Digital Learning Hub in Esch-Belval for a day dedicated to embedding quality throughout the software delivery pipeline.

About the 6th edition

The 6th Luxembourg Software Testing Event brought together over 280 testing and quality professionals for a full day dedicated to the theme of Continuous Quality. The event explored how modern organisations are embedding quality throughout their software delivery pipelines, from requirements to production monitoring.

Held at the Digital Learning Hub in Esch-Belval, LSTE 2024 opened with 3 hands-on morning tutorials, followed by keynote sessions, live demos, and a dedicated exhibition area where sponsors showcased their latest tools and services.

Standout moments included a provocative keynote on the death of the traditional QA role, a live AI-assisted testing demonstration that drew standing-room-only attendance, and a candid panel debate on whether "shift left" has gone too far.

Key takeaways

What the room was talking about.

Continuous quality is a culture

The consensus from LSTE 2024: continuous quality is not a tooling problem; it's a cultural transformation that starts with leadership and requires shared ownership across all teams.

Automation is a means, not an end

Multiple speakers challenged the automation-first mindset. Effective test automation must be selective, maintained, and always in service of a broader quality strategy rather than pursued for its own sake.

Production is the new testing environment

With canary deployments, feature flags, and real-user monitoring becoming standard, the line between testing and production monitoring is blurring, and testers must adapt.
